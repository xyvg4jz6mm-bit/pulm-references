Vent at the SNF

Mechanical ventilation, weaning & airway management · Skilled Nursing Facility point-of-care tool

Lung-protective default: Vt 6–8 mL/kg PBW, plateau ≤ 30 cmH₂O, driving pressure ≤ 14 cmH₂O — recommended across ARDS and non-ARDS patients.8,3
VCV  Volume-Controlled (A/C)

Set a fixed tidal volume; airway pressure varies with compliance and resistance. Every breath — patient- or time-triggered — delivers the full set Vt.

Clinician setsVt, RR, flow, FiO₂, PEEP
VariablePIP, plateau pressure
AdvantageGuaranteed tidal volume
Watch outPressures spike if compliance drops (plug, bronchospasm)
PCV  Pressure-Controlled

Set an inspiratory pressure; decelerating flow, Vt varies with mechanics.

Clinician setsInsp pressure, RR, Ti (or I:E), FiO₂, PEEP
VariableTidal volume, flow
AdvantageCaps peak pressure; better gas distribution
Watch outVt not guaranteed — monitor closely
PCV-VG  PRVC / VC+ / AutoFlow

Decelerating-flow breaths with a volume target; ventilator auto-adjusts inspiratory pressure breath-to-breath to hit the set Vt.

Clinician setsTarget Vt, RR, Ti, FiO₂, PEEP, Pmax
VariableInsp pressure (auto)
AdvantageConsistent Vt, adapts to compliance
Watch outCan mask worsening mechanics — set Pmax appropriately
PSV  Pressure Support — spontaneous

Patient triggers every breath; set pressure augments each effort, flow-cycled to expiration. The workhorse weaning mode.

Clinician setsPS level, FiO₂, PEEP
VariableVt, RR, Ti, flow
AdvantagePatient-driven; muscle conditioning; weaning
Watch outNo guaranteed minute ventilation — set apnea backup
SIMV  Synchronized Intermittent Mandatory

Set mandatory breaths synchronized to effort; spontaneous breaths between (± PS). Weaning by dropping the rate is less preferred than PSV/TC trials — may prolong weaning.7

Clinician setsMandatory RR, Vt/pressure, PS, FiO₂, PEEP
VariableTotal RR, spontaneous Vt

Daily maintenance, VAP prevention, and the mechanics you monitor every shift.

VAP / VAE Prevention Bundle2

  • Head of bed elevated 30–45°
  • Daily readiness assessment + spontaneous awakening / breathing trials
  • Minimize sedation (avoid benzodiazepines where possible)
  • Oral care with regular antiseptic; subglottic suction if available
  • Maintain cuff pressure 20–30 cmH₂O to limit micro-aspiration
  • Early mobilization; VTE and stress-ulcer prophylaxis per protocol

Humidification

  • All trach patients need airway humidification — the trach bypasses the nose
  • On vent: heated humidifier preferred for prolonged use, or HME
  • On trach collar: heated aerosol or large-volume nebulizer
  • Avoid HME with thick/copious secretions, high minute ventilation, or temp < 32°C
  • Inadequate humidification → mucus plugging (can be rapidly fatal)

Auto-PEEP (Intrinsic PEEP)

Suspect: air trapping, expiratory flow not reaching zero, trigger difficulty, hemodynamic compromise. Common in COPD/asthma, high RR, high Vt, short expiratory time.

  • Reduce RR (lengthen expiratory time)
  • Reduce tidal volume
  • Increase inspiratory flow (shortens Ti)
  • Treat bronchospasm (bronchodilators)
  • Apply external PEEP ≤ 80% of measured auto-PEEP to ease triggering

Progressive Trach-Collar Trials (preferred)

Step 130–60 min × 2/day, humidified O₂ to SpO₂ ≥ 92%
Step 2↑ 30–60 min/trial; ≥ 2 h full rest between; vent overnight
Step 3At 4–6 h, consolidate to one longer daily trial
Milestone≥ 12 h spontaneous × 2 consecutive days
ThenAdvance toward 24-h ventilator-free

Gradual PS Reduction (alternative)

↓ PS by 2 cmH₂O q12–24 h as tolerated → target PS 5–8 cmH₂O → transition to trach-collar trials. Consider for patients anxious about disconnection. SIMV rate-reduction is least preferred.

Diaphragm ultrasound (adjunct): thickening fraction cutoff ~30–36% and excursion ≥ 10–14 mm predict success; useful when diaphragm dysfunction is suspected (≈29% of ventilated patients).12

Suctioning Best Practice

  • Indication-based, not routine (audible/visible secretions, rhonchi, weak cough, SpO₂ drop, distress)
  • Closed in-line preferred — preserves PEEP/FiO₂, lowers infection risk
  • Pre-oxygenate 100% O₂ × 30–60 s; suction on withdrawal only
  • Limit each pass to 10–15 s; catheter ≤ ½ the trach inner diameter
  • Routine saline instillation not recommended

Cuff Management

  • Inflated during mechanical ventilation to maintain seal
  • Deflated for speaking valve, capping, upper-airway patency checks
  • Manometer target 20–30 cmH₂O; < 20 → aspiration/leak; > 30 → mucosal ischemia, stenosis
  • Suction above cuff + oropharynx before deflation

Speaking Valve (Passy-Muir)

Never place with the cuff inflated — creates a complete airway obstruction. Cuff must be fully deflated.

Prerequisites

  • Cuff fully deflated & tolerated without desaturation
  • No complete upper-airway obstruction
  • Adequate consciousness & manageable secretions

Benefits

  • Restores voice; restores subglottic pressure (may reduce aspiration)
  • Improves cough, smell/taste; may facilitate weaning

Start 15–30 min trials; remove immediately for distress, stridor, or desaturation.

Consider decannulation once fully liberated (trach collar / room air ≥ 24 h) with resolved indication.

Alarm troubleshooting and patient-ventilator dyssynchrony.

High-Pressure Alarm
  • Mucus plug / secretions → suction; saline if thick
  • Bronchospasm (wheeze) → bronchodilator; notify provider
  • Biting / coughing → reassure; bite block if ETT
  • Water in circuit → drain condensate
  • Kinked tubing → straighten
  • Pneumothorax (absent breath sounds, tracheal deviation, hypotension) → emergency, notify immediately
  • Worsening compliance → check Pplat/driving pressure, CXR
  • Dyssynchrony → adjust trigger/flow/mode; treat pain/anxiety
Low-Pressure / Disconnect Alarm
  • Circuit disconnection → reconnect
  • Cuff leak → reinflate to 20–30 cmH₂O; change tube if persistent
  • Circuit leak → replace components, tighten connections
  • Trach dislodgement → mature stoma: reinsert; fresh trach (< 7 d): do NOT reinsert — bag-mask & call emergency airway
Low Tidal-Volume Alarm
  • Cuff leak → reinflate/replace tube
  • Circuit leak → repair/replace
  • ↓ patient effort (spont modes) → stimulate; consider A/C
  • Worsening compliance (PCV, PIP unchanged but Vt dropping) → notify provider; find cause

DOPE — sudden distress on the ventilator: Displacement · Obstruction · Pneumothorax · Equipment. When in doubt, disconnect and bag manually.

Cannot Ventilate Through Trach — Steps

  1. Remove inner cannula → attempt ventilation
  2. Suction through trach → attempt ventilation
  3. Still failing: deflate cuff → remove trach tube
  4. Mature stoma (> 7 d): reinsert same size; if fail, one size smaller; if fail, ETT through stoma
  5. Immature stoma (< 7 d): do NOT blind-reinsert — cover stoma, bag-mask via face, call emergency airway
  6. Unable by any route → CPR protocol, call 911

Tracheostomy Bleeding

  • Minor (stoma) → gentle pressure; assess granulation; notify
  • Moderate (tracheal) → suction; inflate cuff to tamponade; notify urgently
  • Massive / pulsatile → suspect tracheoinnominate fistula: inflate cuff maximally; if continues, remove tube & apply digital pressure through stoma against posterior sternum (Utley); call 911 — emergent surgery

Bedside Emergency Kit — every trach patient

  • Bag-valve device with trach adapter AND face mask
  • Spare trach — same size AND one size smaller
  • Obturator; 10 mL syringe; water-soluble lubricant
  • Suction (portable + wall) & catheters
  • Independent O₂ source; scissors to cut ties

When to Escalate / Transfer

  • SpO₂ < 88% despite FiO₂ > 0.60 + PEEP optimization
  • New hemodynamic instability or suspected pneumothorax
  • Trach dislodgement in immature stoma (< 7 d)
  • Massive trach bleeding (tracheoinnominate fistula)
  • Acute mental-status change
  • New fever + purulent secretions + worsening O₂ (VAP/VAE)
  • Vent malfunction unresolved; Pplat persistently > 30
  • Cannot ventilate/oxygenate after DOPE workup
