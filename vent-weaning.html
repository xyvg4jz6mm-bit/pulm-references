Vent Weaning — Trach Collar Protocol

Evidence-based liberation for tracheostomized patients · SNF / LTACH

Bottom line: Progressive tracheostomy collar (TC) trials ("sprinting") outperform gradual pressure-support reduction — median weaning 15 vs 19 days, success 53% vs 45% (Jubran 2013, JAMA).

Why TC trials win

Direct unloading of the diaphragm during trials, ventilator rest overnight, and a clear binary endpoint. Benefit is most pronounced in the "late-failure" group — patients who tolerate ≥12 h screening before first failure (HR 3.33 for successful weaning vs PS, p=.005).

Supporting data

Cleveland Clinic retrospective (2024): daily TC trials associated with 43% higher likelihood of successful liberation (HR 1.43, 95% CI 1.05–1.94). ATS/ACCP guideline strongly recommends protocolized weaning over physician-judgment-alone.

Distress signals: RR > 35 sustained, SpO₂ < 90%, HR change > 20%, accessory muscle use, diaphoresis, agitation. Return to prior PS level and reattempt after ≥2 h of full support.

Once patient sustains 5+ days off the ventilator.

1 · Secretion assessment

≤ 1 suctioning every 4 h over a 12-h window suggests readiness.

2 · Swallow evaluation

50 mL water drink test with cuff deflated. Assesses aspiration risk before capping.

3 · Occlusion / capping test

Cap the trach with cuff deflated for 5 minutes to rule out tracheal obstruction. Progress to prolonged capping trial.

4 · Decannulate

If tolerating capping 24 h or meeting the suctioning-frequency criterion — remove the trach.

Post-decannulation: monitor 24–48 h for stridor, work of breathing, secretion management, and need for replacement airway.
